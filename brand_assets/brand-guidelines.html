08

Voice & Tone

We write like a knowledgeable neighbor — warm, direct, and reassuring. Never corporate, never salesy. Every word should make the customer feel that help is already on the way.

✓ Say this
"We'll have someone there today."
Direct, confident, action-oriented
✗ Not this
"Our team will endeavor to schedule a technician at your earliest convenience."
Corporate, vague, and cold
✓ Say this
"No fix, no fee — your diagnosis is always free."
Simple, reassuring, memorable
✗ Not this
"Complimentary diagnostic assessment included with all service appointments."
Jargon-heavy, feels like a contract
✓ Say this
"Your washer's acting up? We've seen it all — and fixed it all."
Relatable, confident, slightly playful
✗ Not this
"Leverage our expertise for optimized appliance performance outcomes."
Meaningless buzzwords
Writing principles
🏠 Home first — speak to homeowners, not to business clients. Use "your home," "your washer," "your family."
⚡ Action-led — start CTAs with strong verbs: Book, Schedule, Call, Fix, Get.
🤝 Honest — never overpromise. "Most repairs" not "all repairs." "Usually same-day" not "always."
✂️ Short sentences — aim for under 20 words per sentence. Break long ideas into two lines.
